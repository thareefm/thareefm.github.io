I spend my time split between photography, film-making, economic research and financial analysis, whilst shuffling between Singapore and Sri Lanka. I formally studied mathematics and economics and currently head economics at a leading investment consultancy cum hedge fund in South Asia.

I think you can always go back to a good photograph. You can look at it, absorb it, and do this time and time again. Because if framed purposefully it has the power to take you to that frozen slice of time. Photography is food for my soul.

Economics was a subject that always intrigued me. After picking up a copy of Muhammad Yunus's book 'Banker to the Poor', I was astonished how one could leverage a cultrual norm in order to empower a community through credit markets. It became apparent to me that economics was essentially social engineering. Even the success's of todays economic policies are hingent upon their ability to affect decisions at the household level. Essentially an economic policy, or pricing strategy seeks to construct an incentive structure to motivate household decisions in accordance to what policy makers, or entreprenurs deem appropriate.
